We Are Not Apex Predators, We Are Apex “Bullshitters”

Justice isn’t a natural law; it’s a human invention. Explore why rape, genocide, and exploitation persist not despite our systems, but because we’ve perfected the art of justifying them.

A lion brings down a gazelle. It is brutal, bloody, and efficient. There is no hate in the act, just hunger. There is no malice, just biology. The lion does not lie to itself or the gazelle about what is happening. It does not call the gazelle an “enemy of the pride” or claim the hunt is for the “greater good of the ecosystem.” It is pure, honest predation.

Now, watch us. Watch a corporation pump toxins into a river, sicken a community, and then launch a million-dollar public relations campaign touting their commitment to sustainability. Watch a country invade its neighbour, cite fabricated evidence of weapons of mass destruction, and call it a “liberation.” See the difference?

We are not apex predators. We are something far more complex and far more vile. We are Apex Bullshitters. Our genius wasn’t in rising above nature’s brutality; it was in perfecting the art of disguising it with lies, legal frameworks, and self-righteous propaganda.

Section 1: The Law of the Jungle (The Honest Truth)

Nature is brutally, refreshingly honest. Its laws are simple: survive, reproduce, consume energy. A tsunami doesn’t issue a press release before it obliterates a coastline. A virus doesn’t claim it’s spreading for “your own good.” It operates on cause and effect, not narrative and spin.

There is no justice here. There is only consequence.

Real-Life Example: In the animal kingdom, when a new male lion takes over a pride, he often kills the cubs sired by his predecessor. It is a horrific act, but it is not evil. It is a biological strategy to bring the females into ovulation faster so he can pass on his own genes. It is amoral efficiency. There is no bullshit. It just is.

Section 2: The Human Loophole: We Invented the Rulebook (And How to Break It)

Our big brains and capacity for language were meant to be our salvation. We could imagine a better world and cooperate to build it. We invented justice, law, and morality as a shield against nature’s chaos.

But we quickly discovered that he who controls the language, controls the reality. We didn’t just invent the rules; we invented how to break them while sounding like we’re following them.

The social contract was never about perfect justice. It was about creating a more stable system for us to accumulate power. Our gift for storytelling became a weapon to justify our most base, natural instincts.

Section 3: Why Bad Things Happen: The Game is Rigged

This is the heart of it. Why do rape, loot, and genocide happen? Because our systems are not designed to prevent them; they are often designed to enable them under a new name.

  • Rape is not an animalistic aberration in our civilized world. It is the ultimate act of predatory domination, one that our justice system has historically downplayed, dismissed, and buried in bureaucratic bullshit. Victims are put on trial, their stories picked apart, while the predator’s “potential” is protected. We don’t call it predation; we call it “a mistake,” “he said/she said,” or we just don’t call it anything at all, silencing the victim with shame — a human-invented concept that nature doesn’t recognize.
  • Loot is not theft when you do it with a pen. The 2008 global financial crisis was not caused by natural disaster, but by bankers who repackaged toxic debt into complex instruments, had rating agencies label them “AAA” (the bullshit), and sold them to the world. They looted the global economy, destroyed millions of lives, and were then deemed “too big to fail,” receiving bailouts while ordinary people lost their homes. This wasn’t predation; it was bullshittery of the highest order. They didn’t break the rules; they wrote the rules of the game to ensure they would win.
  • Genocide is never just killing. It is killing preceded by a sophisticated campaign of bullshit. The Holocaust didn’t start with gas chambers; it started with words. It started with propaganda that labeled Jews “vermin” and “a disease.” The Rwandan Genocide saw Tutsis called “cockroaches.” This language is the crucial, uniquely human step. It is the bullshit that dehumanizes the victim, turning acts of natural brutality into a “necessary cleansing” or “ethnic conflict.” It’s nature’s violence, premeditated and justified with a microphone and a manifesto.

Section 4: The Apex Bullshitter

An apex predator kills. An apex bullshitter creates a reality where the kill is not just justified, but celebrated.

Our intelligence and language, which we thought would save us, became the ultimate tools for deception. We are the only species that can commit an atrocity and then write a speech, pass a law, or run an ad campaign to explain why it was actually noble.

Real-Life Example: Consider colonialism. It wasn’t just about stronger nations conquering weaker ones (that’s natural). It was about conquering them and calling it “The White Man’s Burden.” It was about framing the theft of land, resources, and lives as a “civilizing mission.” They didn’t just take; they claimed they were giving: giving religion, giving culture, giving order. This is the pinnacle of apex bullshittery — inflicting violence and convincing yourself you are the victim’s savior.

A modern corporation engages in union-busting, not to crush workers’ rights, but to “preserve workplace harmony and flexibility.” A government engages in mass surveillance, not to erode liberty, but for “your safety and security.”

We have mastered the art of taking the oldest, darkest, most natural impulses — greed, violence, dominance — and dressing them up in a suit of legal, political, and social justification. We don’t transcend our nature; we gaslight ourselves and the world about it.

Conclusion: The Uncomfortable Truth and The Fight

So here is the uncomfortable truth: Nature has no justice. Our human version is a tool, and it is a tool that is most often used by the powerful to bullshit the powerless.

We are not noble apex predators. We are hypocrites. We are liars. We are Apex Bullshitters.

But this rage you feel — this disgust at the hypocrisy — is not a reason to give up. It is the only thing that proves the concept of real justice has meaning.

The project of humanity is no longer the naive dream of building a perfect utopia. That is a fantasy. The project is the gritty, unglamorous, and essential work of calling out the bullshit.

It is the work of journalists uncovering corruption, of whistleblowers risking everything, of activists demanding answers, and of ordinary people refusing to accept the official narrative. It is the exhausting fight to hold a mirror up to our species and force it to see the gap between its words and its actions.

We may be Apex Bullshitters, but we are also the only species capable of smelling our own crap. That ability to recognize our own hypocrisy — that revulsion for the lie — is the thin, fragile thread that our entire concept of justice hangs by.
